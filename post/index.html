Chemotaxis is the movement of cells along a chemical gradient. From Escherichia coli to Homo sapiens, it is a highly conserved behaviour that enables directed cell movement. For motile unicellular organisms, chemotaxis enables movement towards resources like nutrition and space and away from toxins or competitors. For multicellular organisms like metazoans, chemotaxis drives coordinated cell movements during development, such as gastrulation, germ cell migration, and neural crest cell migration.
